## Design Context

### Users
Construction clients in the Philippines (Iloilo City and beyond): property developers, business owners commissioning commercial builds, homeowners, and government procurement officers. They visit from a desktop or phone to vet ARSD's credibility before making a call or submitting an inquiry. Trust and competence are the primary signals they need. Context: professional, goal-oriented — they're comparison-shopping contractors, not browsing casually.

### Brand Personality
Grounded. Proven. Capable.
The company has been operating since 1998 with PCAB licensing, SEC registration, and PhilGEPS supplier status. The tone should feel like the firm handshake of a contractor who has built 500+ projects — no hype, no flash. Restrained confidence over self-promotion.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark industrial: deep charcoal (#111111 / #1c1c1c), warm cream text (#f0ede8), muted warm grey (#a09890), arsd-red (#DC2626) as a sharp, sparing accent. Display font: Barlow Condensed (uppercase, weight 700–800). Body: Figtree. Uppercase section headings with tight tracking. Flat grid layouts with dividing lines instead of card boxes. No glassmorphism, no gradient text, no decorative glows.

Anti-references: SaaS landing pages with gradient hero text, generic "white card + icon + heading + text" grids, dark-mode dashboards with cyan/purple glows.

Theme: dark (construction crews work in the field; site visitors expect solidity and weight, not corporate cleanliness).

### Design Principles
1. **Credibility through restraint** — every design decision should say "we've been doing this for 25 years," not "we just launched a startup."
2. **Flat over boxed** — use dividing lines and whitespace to separate content; avoid wrapping everything in card containers.
3. **Red is reserved** — arsd-red appears only on primary CTAs and key accent moments. Overuse dilutes its signal.
4. **Uppercase as structure** — Barlow Condensed uppercase headings carry the visual weight; body copy stays sentence-case and readable.
5. **Consistency across pages** — the About Us page currently uses a completely different visual language (white background, glassmorphism, gradient blobs, border-left stripes). All pages must share the same dark industrial system.
